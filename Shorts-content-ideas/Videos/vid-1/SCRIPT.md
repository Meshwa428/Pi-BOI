# pi-boi — Video 1 Script
**"I'm building a handheld Game Boy. With a $15 computer."**
*45–60 seconds | Hook-style Reel | Funny + Casual | No face cam — hands + voiceover*

---

## HOOK (0:00 – 0:05)
*[Overhead or close-up shot — hands opening the antistatic bag, slowly pulling out the Pi Zero 2 W. Hold it up between two fingers so the size is obvious.]*

> "So I decided to build my own Game Boy."

*[Beat. Hold the board still. Let the viewer take in how tiny it is.]*

> "...using this."

*[Set it down gently on the desk. Just let it sit there.]*

> "Yeah."

---

## SETUP (0:05 – 0:20)
*[Close-up shot of the Pi Zero 2 W lying flat on the desk. Slowly rotate it with one finger to show both sides.]*

> "This is a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W.
> Quad core processor. Wi-Fi. Bluetooth.
> All of that — on something the size of a stick of gum."

*[Pull back slightly so the whole board is in frame. Just let it sit there looking tiny.]*

> "And I'm going to turn it into a fully working handheld.
> Retro gaming, lossless audio player, FM radio…
> the whole thing."

*[One finger tap on the board.]*

> "Somehow."

---

## THE DEMO — FM TRANSMISSION (0:20 – 0:45)
*[Cut to: overhead shot — Pi Zero on desk, single jumper wire poking out of a GPIO pin. Keep it messy and real.]*

> "But here's the part that actually broke my brain."

*[Hand enters frame, picks up just the jumper wire end and holds it up — one wire dangling.]*

> "One wire. One GPIO pin. That's it."

*[Cut to wider shot showing both the Pi and a phone/radio nearby. Hand reaches over and tunes the FM app/radio to the frequency. Music starts coming out.]*

> "This thing is now an FM radio station."

*[Hold on the shot — let the music play for 3–4 seconds. No talking. Let it land.]*

> "My Pi Zero 2 W is currently broadcasting clearer than
> half the actual FM stations in my city.
> I don't know whether to be impressed or concerned."

---

## CLOSE / CTA (0:45 – 0:55)
*[Cut back to tight overhead shot — just the Pi Zero 2 W alone on the desk.]*

> "This is episode one.
> I have no idea how many videos this is going to take.
> But we're building the whole thing —
> from this one tiny board…
> to something that actually fits in your pocket."

*[Hand reaches in and picks it up — hold it flat on the palm so the size is visible one last time.]*

> "Follow along. This is going to get interesting."

*[Slowly close the hand. Cut to black.]*

## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **The "Yeah." moment** — set the board down slowly and just let it sit. The stillness IS the joke. Don't rush to the next line.
- **Rotating the board** — do it slowly with one finger, like you're presenting evidence. Both sides of the Pi look impressively packed for the size.
- **The single jumper wire** — hold it up high, let it dangle. The visual contrast between "one floppy wire" and "FM radio station" is everything.
- **Let the music play 3–4 seconds in silence** — this is the most important beat in the whole video. Fight the urge to talk over it.
- **"Impressed or concerned"** — deliver it flat, no laugh in the voice.
- **The closing hand close** — slow, deliberate. Ends on a confident note without needing a face or a smile.
- **Shoot everything overhead or close macro distance** — no face needed at any point, the board and hands carry the whole thing.
