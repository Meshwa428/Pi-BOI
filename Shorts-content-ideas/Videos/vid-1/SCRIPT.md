# pi-boi — Video 1 Script + Delivery Guide
**"I'm building a handheld Game Boy. With a $15 computer."**
*45–60 seconds | Hook-style Reel | Funny + Casual | No face cam — hands + voiceover*

---

## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Every line has three things:
- The **line itself** — what you say
- **[shot]** — what's on screen
- **〈delivery〉** — exactly how to say it

Read the delivery note before recording each line. Record each section separately if needed — you can stitch it in editing.

---

## GENERAL TONE FOR THE WHOLE VIDEO

Think of it like this: you're texting a friend about something cool you found, but out loud.
- **Not excited. Not hype. Just... matter of fact.**
- The comedy comes from being completely calm about impressive things.
- Speak slower than you think you need to. Seriously. Slow. Down.
- Leave gaps. Silence is not dead air — it's a punchline.
- If you mess up a line, just pause 2 seconds and say it again. Easy to edit out.

## HOOK (0:00 – 0:05)

*[Overhead shot — hands opening the antistatic bag, pulling out the Pi Zero 2 W. Hold it up between two fingers.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "So I decided to build my own Game Boy."

〈 Casual. Relaxed. Like you're starting a sentence in the middle of a conversation.
  NOT excited. NOT announcing. Just... stating it.
  Normal talking speed. No emphasis on any particular word. 〉

---

*[Beat — hold the board still for 1 full second. Let them look at it.]*

**LINE:**
> "...using this."

〈 Drop your voice slightly here. Quieter than the first line.
  The "..." is real — actually pause for half a second before saying it.
  Say it like you're showing someone something and letting them figure it out.
  No upward inflection at the end. Flat. 〉

---

*[Set it down on the desk slowly.]*

**LINE:**
> "Yeah."

〈 THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT LINE IN THE VIDEO.
  One word. That's it. Don't add anything.
  Say it like you've just accepted something mildly unfortunate.
  Flat tone. Slightly tired. Like "yep, this is what I'm doing with my life."
  Then go completely silent for 1 full second before the next section.
  Do NOT laugh after it. Do NOT explain it. Just let it sit. 〉

## SETUP (0:05 – 0:20)

*[Close-up of the Pi Zero lying flat. Slowly rotate it with one finger.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "This is a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W."

〈 Normal pace. Like reading a label. No drama yet. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "Quad core processor. Wi-Fi. Bluetooth."

〈 Slightly faster here — like rattling off a list you've memorised.
  Short punchy sentences. Each one lands separately.
  QUAD core. [tiny beat] Wi-Fi. [tiny beat] Bluetooth. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "All of that — on something the size of a stick of gum."

〈 Slow back down for this line. Let "stick of gum" land.
  Slight pause after the dash "All of that —" before continuing.
  Don't emphasise "stick of gum" too hard — understatement is funnier than excitement. 〉

---

*[Pull back so the whole board is visible.]*

**LINE:**
> "And I'm going to turn it into a fully working handheld."

〈 Confident. Calm. Like you've already decided and there's no going back.
  Steady pace. No wavering. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "Retro gaming, lossless audio player, FM radio…"

〈 Same rattling-off-a-list energy as before.
  Trail off slightly on "FM radio…" — let the "…" actually trail in your voice.
  Like you're still thinking of things to add. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "the whole thing."

〈 Short. Final. Like closing a door.
  Slight drop in volume. Matter of fact. 〉

---

*[One finger tap on the board.]*

**LINE:**
> "Somehow."

〈 PAUSE after "the whole thing." for a full second before this word.
  Say it quietly. Almost to yourself.
  This is the second laugh — it's the honest admission that you have no idea what you're doing.
  Do NOT smile in your voice here. Keep it dry and flat. 〉

## THE DEMO — FM TRANSMISSION (0:20 – 0:45)

*[Cut to overhead — Pi Zero on desk, single jumper wire poking out of GPIO pin.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "But here's the part that actually broke my brain."

〈 This is your gear shift — slightly more energy than the setup, but still not hype.
  Emphasise "actually" just a little — "that ACTUALLY broke my brain."
  This signals to the viewer: pay attention, something's about to happen. 〉

---

*[Hand picks up just the wire end, holds it up dangling.]*

**LINE:**
> "One wire."

〈 Pause after this. Let them look at the wire. 〉

**LINE:**
> "One GPIO pin."

〈 Same energy. Short. Punchy. 〉

**LINE:**
> "That's it."

〈 Flat. Final. Put the wire down or let it dangle.
  Then go silent for half a second. 〉

---

*[Hand tunes the phone/radio to the frequency. Music starts playing.]*

**LINE:**
> "This thing is now an FM radio station."

〈 Calm. Matter of fact. Like you're reading a weather report.
  The comedy here is NOT in your voice — it's in the contrast between
  how boring you sound and how insane what just happened actually is.
  Say it at normal speed. No emphasis. Then STOP TALKING. 〉

---

*[SILENCE — let the music play for 3–4 full seconds. Don't say anything.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "My Pi Zero 2 W is currently broadcasting clearer than half the actual FM stations in my city."

〈 Come back in casually — like you forgot you were recording for a second.
  Medium pace. Slightly conversational.
  Emphasise "clearer" just a touch. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "I don't know whether to be impressed or concerned."

〈 THIS IS THE LAUGH LINE. Deliver it completely flat.
  No smile in your voice. No laugh at the end.
  Say it like you genuinely haven't decided yet.
  Pause for half a second after "concerned" — then move on. 〉

## CLOSE / CTA (0:45 – 0:55)

*[Cut to tight overhead — just the Pi alone on the desk.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "This is episode one."

〈 Calm. Grounded. Slight drop in pace — this is the wind-down. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "I have no idea how many videos this is going to take."

〈 Honest. Slightly self-aware. Don't rush this line — it's relatable and people will connect with it.
  Slight emphasis on "no idea." 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "But we're building the whole thing —"

〈 Confident again. Small uptick in energy. "We" is intentional — it includes the audience. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "from this one tiny board…"

〈 Trail off here. Let the "…" breathe in your voice. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "to something that actually fits in your pocket."

〈 Land this line cleanly. Normal pace. Ends on a satisfying note. 〉

---

*[Hand picks up the Pi — holds it flat on the palm.]*

**LINE:**
> "Follow along. This is going to get interesting."

〈 Two short sentences. Confident but not salesy.
  Don't go up in pitch at the end like a question.
  Keep it flat and genuine. Like a friend telling you to stick around.
  Then go quiet. Let the shot hold for 1 second before cutting. 〉

## RECORDING TIPS

- **Record in a quiet room.** Even a tiny echo will make the voiceover sound amateur. Closets with clothes are surprisingly good recording spots.
- **Record each section 3 times.** Pick the best take in editing. You don't have to get it perfect first try.
- **Don't record straight after waking up or when stressed.** Your voice will sound tighter than you want.
- **The pauses feel too long when you're recording them.** They won't in the edit. Trust the silence.
- **If a line sounds too "presenter-y" or fake, just say it again more quietly and slower.** Quieter almost always sounds more natural.
- **Water before recording.** Dry mouth is audible.
