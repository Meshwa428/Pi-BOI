# pi-boi — Video 2 Script + Delivery Guide
**"Meet the parts. Most of them."**
*45–60 seconds | Hook-style Reel | Funny + Casual | No face cam — hands + voiceover*

---

## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Every line has three things:
- The **line itself** — what you say
- **[shot]** — what's on screen
- **〈delivery〉** — exactly how to say it

---

## GENERAL TONE FOR THIS VIDEO

Same as Video 1 — calm, dry, matter of fact. The comedy in this video comes from two "that's what she said" moments, so everything around them needs to be played completely straight. The flatter you are, the harder they land. Think of yourself as a very serious product reviewer who keeps accidentally saying things.

## HOOK (0:00 – 0:05)

*[Overhead shot — all parts laid out flat in a clean row. Pi Zero, DAC, amplifier, FM receiver, tactile switches. Neat, satisfying layout.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "Okay so here's everything that's going into pi-boi."

〈 Casual opener. Like you're showing a friend your desk.
  Medium pace. No drama. Slightly upbeat compared to Video 1's opener —
  this one is more "here we go" energy. 〉

---

*[Hand slowly sweeps left to right across all the parts.]*

**LINE:**
> "...most of it."

〈 Real pause before this — let the hand finish the sweep first.
  Drop your voice slightly. Quieter than the first line.
  The "..." is real. Actually wait half a second before saying it. 〉

---

*[Hand stops mid-air.]*

**LINE:**
> "The rest is on its way. I think."

〈 "The rest is on its way." — flat, normal pace.
  Then pause for half a beat before "I think."
  "I think" should sound like you just remembered you're not sure.
  Slight drop in confidence on those two words. Don't play it up — just let it be uncertain. 〉

## THE PARTS (0:05 – 0:40)

*[Hand picks up the Pi Zero 2 W.]*

---

**LINE:**
> "You already know this one."

〈 Short. Dismissive. Almost bored.
  Set it back down quickly — like it doesn't need introduction.
  This is a reward for people who watched Video 1. Deliver it like an inside joke. 〉

---

*[Set it down. Pick up the UDA1334A DAC.]*

**LINE:**
> "This is the DAC. Digital to analogue converter."

〈 Normal pace. Reading-a-label energy. No excitement yet. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "Basically — this is why the audio on this thing is going to sound actually good"

〈 Slight pause after "Basically —"
  Emphasise "actually good" just a little — like you're surprised it's possible. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "instead of whatever the Pi normally sounds like."

〈 Trail off slightly. Vague and dismissive about the Pi's onboard audio.
  Like you don't even want to think about it. 〉

---

*[Set it down. Pick up the PAM8403 amplifier — it's tiny.]*

**LINE:**
> "This is the amplifier. Class D stereo. Powers the speakers."

〈 Same rattling-off energy as the spec list in Video 1.
  Short punchy sentences landing one after another.
  AMPLIFIER. [beat] Class D stereo. [beat] Powers the speakers. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "It's the size of a postage stamp."

〈 Flat. Observational. Like you just noticed it again and it's mildly offensive. 〉

---

*[Hold the amp next to the Pi Zero — both tiny, side by side.]*

**LINE:**
> "Everything in this build is aggressively small."

〈 Slow this line down. Say it like you're processing a problem.
  Let "aggressively small" land — slight emphasis on "aggressively."
  Then go completely silent. Let them stare at both tiny boards for 1 full second. 〉

---

*[Beat — silence.]*

**LINE:**
> "...that's what she said."

〈 THIS IS LAUGH #1.
  The "..." is real — wait a genuine half second before saying it.
  Say it quietly. Almost under your breath. Like it slipped out.
  Completely flat tone. No laugh in your voice. No smile.
  Then move on immediately — don't wait for the laugh, just go. 〉

---

*[Set both down. Pick up the RDA5807M FM receiver — it's the smallest thing on the desk.]*

**LINE:**
> "This is the FM receiver chip."

〈 Back to normal presenter tone — the joke is over, you're professional again. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "Picks up radio stations. Has RDS support —"

〈 Rattling off specs again. Medium pace. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "so it'll show the station name and song title on screen."

〈 Slightly slower here — this is actually impressive, let it register. 〉

---

*[Hold it close to camera. It's comically tiny.]*

**LINE:**
> "It costs about two dollars."

〈 Pause before this line for half a second.
  Say it completely deadpan. Like you're reading a receipt.
  No inflection up or down. Just a fact.
  Then set it down and move on. 〉

---

*[Pick up the 5-way tactile switch.]*

**LINE:**
> "And this — this is the joystick."

〈 Slight pause after "And this —" before repeating it.
  The repetition sounds natural — like you're figuring out how to describe it. 〉

---

**LINE:**
> "Five directions plus a click. This is how you're going to play games on it."

〈 Confident. Matter of fact. Medium pace. 〉

---

*[Click it a few times — let the tactile sound come through on mic.]*

**LINE:**
> "It's so satisfying to play with."

〈 Say this genuinely. Calm, sincere, slightly distracted —
  like you forgot you were recording because you started clicking it.
  Normal pace. Let the clicks be audible before and after the line.
  Then pause. Full 1 second of silence. 〉

---

*[Beat — silence.]*

**LINE:**
> "...again, that's what she said."

〈 THIS IS LAUGH #2 — and it's bigger than the first because of "again."
  Same delivery as the first one — quiet, almost to yourself, flat tone.
  The word "again" does all the work. It implies this keeps happening.
  Do NOT laugh after it. Do NOT explain it. Just let it sit for half a second then go. 〉

## RECORDING TIPS FOR THIS VIDEO

- **The two "that's what she said" lines need identical energy** — both quiet, both flat, both slightly delayed. If one sounds more played-up than the other, it breaks the joke.
- **The clicking sound is part of the script** — make sure your mic is close enough to pick it up. Record the clicking separately if needed and layer it in editing.
- **"It costs about two dollars"** — this line needs the longest silence before it. The pause is what makes it funny.
- **If either joke makes you laugh while recording** — stop, breathe, start that section again. Laughing at your own joke on a voiceover kills it completely.
