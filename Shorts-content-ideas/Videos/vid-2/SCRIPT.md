# pi-boi — Video 2 Script
**"Meet the parts. Most of them."**
*45–60 seconds | Hook-style Reel | Funny + Casual | No face cam — hands + voiceover*

---

## HOOK (0:00 – 0:05)
*[Overhead shot — all the parts laid out flat on the desk in a neat row. Pi Zero, DAC, amplifier, FM receiver, tactile switches. Clean, satisfying layout.]*

> "Okay so here's everything that's going into pi-boi."

*[Hand slowly sweeps across the row.]*

> "...most of it."

*[Hand stops. Pause.]*

> "The rest is on its way. I think."

---

## THE PARTS (0:05 – 0:40)
*[Hand picks up the Pi Zero 2 W first — already familiar from Video 1.]*

> "You already know this one."

*[Set it back down. Pick up the UDA1334A DAC.]*

> "This is the DAC. Digital to analogue converter.
> Basically — this is why the audio on this thing
> is going to sound actually good
> instead of whatever the Pi normally sounds like."

*[Set it down. Pick up the PAM8403 amplifier — it's tiny.]*

> "This is the amplifier.
> Class D stereo. Powers the speakers.
> It's the size of a postage stamp."

*[Hold it next to the Pi Zero for scale — they're similarly tiny.]*

> "Everything in this build is aggressively small."

*[Beat.]*

> "...that's what she said."

*[Set it down. Pick up the RDA5807M FM receiver — even tinier.]*

> "This is the FM receiver chip.
> Picks up radio stations. Has RDS support —
> so it'll show the station name and song title on screen."

*[Hold it close to camera. It's comically small.]*

> "It costs about two dollars."

*[Set it down. Now pick up the 5-way tactile switch.]*

> "And this — this is the joystick.
> Five directions plus a click.
> This is how you're going to play games on it."

*[Click it a few times — satisfying tactile sound.]*

> "It's so satisfying to play with."

*[Beat.]*

> "...again, that's what she said."

## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **The opening sweep** — do it slowly, left to right. Let people actually see each board before the hand moves on.
- **"You already know this one."** — set the Pi down quickly, almost dismissively. It's a callback to Video 1 and rewards people who watched it.
- **Holding the amp next to the Pi for scale** — keep both in frame together. The visual of two tiny boards side by side is funnier than describing it.
- **"I've been clicking it for twenty minutes"** — this is the laugh line. Deliver it like a confession. Because it's probably true.
- **"Next video is going to be loud"** — tease the speaker/amplifier first test. Gives people a reason to follow before the cut.
- **Tactile click sound** — if your mic picks it up, keep it in. That sound does a lot of work.
